## Why this plan exists

2026-06-11, first real human use after a "production-ready" merge (PR #3) broke the core
flow in 30 seconds. Two defects the owner hit on a physical iPhone:

- **D-1 — text loss at silence boundaries.** Spoken text appears, then a 1-2s pause makes
  the NEXT phrase REPLACE it on screen. Diagnosis: the live partial is never committed
  when the recognition task recycles (benign no-speech restart cancels the task; a
  cancelled SFSpeechRecognitionTask delivers no final; the next task's partials overwrite
  the display). The Stop path commits partials — the RESTART path does not. The ~1s
  replay-tail can additionally re-transcribe overlapping audio.
- **D-2 — inverted semantics + fragmentation.** The product must show ONLY dispatcher
  (ATC) transmissions, each as ONE whole permanent block; everything else is filtered.
  Shipped behavior: display-everything-doubtful, suppress-the-rest, in 3-5-word fragments.

## Owner intent (the product, in one paragraph)

The phone listens to EVERYTHING (mic or wired cockpit feed: pilots, cabin, ATC). From all
of it the app identifies DISPATCHER transmissions — anchored by the aircraft's own
callsign and/or by NOT being the enrolled pilot's voice — transcribes each COMPLETE
transmission (glued across short pauses), and renders it as one permanent rectangular
block on screen, exactly like the demo cards look. Pilot speech and irrelevant chatter
never reach the main screen (reviewable filtered list only). Purpose: ATC speaks fast and
unclear; the pilot must SEE the whole instruction without asking to repeat.
